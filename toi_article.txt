	Article heading:  Google honours Dr Herbert Kleber: Here is what you need to know




	Article content:
	Today’s Google Doodle is dedicated to Dr Herbert Kleber who is an American psychiatrist and spent half of his life in developing treatment for substance abuse and reducing the effects of withdrawal. His research and findings changed the way addiction is viewed and helped save millions of lives. Dr Herbert is known for his pioneering work in addiction and its treatment.

Google has dedicated its Doodle to Dr Herbert on the 23rd anniversary of his election to the National Academy of Medicine, which is a volunteer organization that provides medical advice.
 
When Dr Herbert began his career, drug addiction was not a thing of focus in the medical community. His began his research on the subject when he was deployed to a prison hospital in Lexington, Kentucky, where the inmates were being treated for addiction.

He realised that the majority of patients relapsed after getting discharged. He developed ‘evidence-based treatment’ for treating addiction as a medical condition instead of failure of the moral character. Dr Herbert stressed on the importance of research on the subject to prevent relapses in patients.

In his five decade long career, he authored more than 250 papers and articles on addiction. He even mentored generations of researches who later turned out to be leaders in the field of substance abuse.

In 1996, he was elected to be a member of the prestigious Institue of Medicine of the National Academy of Science.

Dr Herbert Kleber passed away on October 5, 2018, at the age of 84.
